Website Evaluations

Below are two website evaluations based on the C.R.A.P. design principles.

C.R.A.P. Principles

  • Contrast
  • Repetition
  • Alignment
  • Proximity

UNC Charlotte Admissions & Financial Aid

This website struggles with several CRAP principles:

  • Contrast: The navigation menu and headings lack strong visual contrast, making important links and sections blend in with the rest of the content.
  • Repetition: Inconsistent font styles and button designs break visual consistency across pages.
  • Alignment: The layout is misaligned, especially in the way text and images are spaced. It makes the content feel scattered and hard to scan.
  • Proximity: Related information is not always grouped together, which affects readability and makes the site feel cluttered.

Apple's Amazon Store

This website shows strong use of CRAP principles:

  • Contrast: Clear text on white backgrounds and bold headings help distinguish key information quickly.
  • Repetition: Consistent use of fonts, buttons, and layout structures maintains a clean and professional look.
  • Alignment: Elements are well-aligned in a grid structure, which helps guide the eye and improves flow.
  • Proximity: Related items (like product info and prices) are grouped together, improving the browsing experience.
